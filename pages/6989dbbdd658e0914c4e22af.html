Environmental Resources

Statement of Function The Walton County Environmental Resource serves as the cornerstone for environmental stewardship within the county, tasked with providing comprehensive environmental support services to various stakeholders including County government, citizens, and federal, state, and local government agencies. At its core, it is dedicated to safeguarding and enhancing the natural resources of Walton County. Through proactive initiatives and collaborative efforts, it addresses a spectrum of environmental issues ranging from conservation and preservation of ecosystems to sustainable development practices. By fostering partnerships and leveraging expertise, the division plays a pivotal role in promoting environmental sustainability, resilience, and harmony between human activities and the natural environment in Walton County. Environmental Resources Departmental Goal, Objectives, and Performance Measures The Environmental Resource Division will continue to help support environmental efforts throughout Walton County including advancements of artificial reefs, involvement in with the Choctawhatchee Bay Estuary Coalition, education and outreach efforts for the Coastal Dune Lakes, management of the Habitat Conservation Plan, management of invasive species within the County right-of-ways and County owned properties, management of the MS4/NPDES Stormwater Management Program, support with regulation of wildlife lighting, regulation of small quantity generators, coordination of derelict vessel removal, maintenance of No Wake Zones, ecotourism input, biological assessments for County related projects and land purchases, well compliance, permitting and mitigation, involvement and coordination of the endangered species, RESTORE/NRDA, involvement in Walton Beautiful, and help solicit grant opportunities associated with water quality, environmental restoration, and ecotourism.
