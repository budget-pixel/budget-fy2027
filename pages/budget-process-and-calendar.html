Financial Structure, Policies, and Process

Budget Process

A simplified guide to how Walton County develops, reviews, adopts, and manages the annual budget.

The annual budget process turns department needs, revenue forecasts, public priorities, Board direction, and Florida statutory requirements into an adopted spending plan for the fiscal year.

Four Phases, One Adopted Budget

Phase 1

Preparation

Departments prepare operating and capital requests based on service needs, staffing, projects, and funding assumptions.

Phase 2

Review

OMB and County Administration evaluate requests against revenue estimates, priorities, fund capacity, and policy guidance.

Phase 3

Workshops

The tentative budget is reviewed publicly with the Board, including funding requests, service levels, and the proposed millage rate.

Phase 4

Adoption

Required public hearings are held before the Board adopts the final millage rate and annual operating budget.

Basis of Budgeting

Walton County prepares and adopts its annual budget in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles and applicable Florida Statutes governing local government budgeting practices.

GAAP

Budgeting Standards

Annual budgets for governmental fund types that are legally required to prepare a budget are adopted on a basis consistent with GAAP.

Control

Encumbrance Accounting

Encumbrances represent commitments for goods or services, including purchase orders and contractual obligations.

Accounting

Modified Accrual Basis

The modified accrual basis is used for budgeting purposes and audited financial statements for governmental funds.

Grants

Grant Revenue Treatment

Grant revenue is generally added to the budget after grant agreements are executed or prior-year grant balances are carried forward.

What Happens During The Process

From requests to recommendations

Budget development begins when departments receive instructions from the Office of Management and Budget. Departments review current programs, service demands, staffing, capital needs, and operating requirements before submitting requests.

OMB reviews requests with County Administration and departments, compares proposed expenditures to available resources, and prepares recommendations for Board consideration.

  • Operating and capital requests are reviewed together.
  • Constitutional Officer budgets are incorporated at required points in the process.
  • Public workshops and hearings provide transparency before adoption.

Budget Implementation & Amendments

Ongoing budget monitoring

After adoption, the budget remains an active financial management tool. The Office of Management and Budget and County departments monitor spending, revenues, appropriations, and amendments throughout the fiscal year.

Budgetary control is established at the fund level for all funds to ensure total fund appropriations remain within approved limits.

Budget amendment process

When circumstances require changes to the approved budget, the Board of County Commissioners may amend the budget by resolution in accordance with Section 129.06, Florida Statutes.

Amendments may be needed for unforeseen expenditures, use of contingency reserves, allocation of unanticipated revenues, grant revenues, or carry-forward funding.
