Financial Structure, Policies, and Process

Budget Process & Calendar

Process Overview

Budget Process at a Glance

Walton County’s annual budget process moves through four major phases before the final budget is adopted.

Budget Preparation

Departments prepare operating and capital requests based on service needs, organizational priorities, and anticipated funding requirements.

Budget Review

OMB and County Administration review departmental requests and evaluate proposed expenditures against projected revenues and operational priorities.

Public Workshop

The tentative budget is presented for Board review and public input, including discussion of the proposed millage rate and service levels.

Budget Adoption

Public hearings are conducted before the Board formally adopts the final millage rate and annual operating budget.

Financial Framework

Basis of Budgeting

Walton County prepares and adopts its annual budget in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and applicable Florida Statutes governing local government budgeting practices.

GAAP Budgeting Standards

Annual budgets for governmental fund types that are legally required to prepare an annual budget are adopted on a basis consistent with GAAP.

Appropriations in all budgeted funds lapse at the end of the fiscal year even if related encumbrances remain outstanding.

Encumbrance Accounting

Encumbrances represent commitments related to unperformed contracts for goods or services, including purchase orders and contractual obligations.

Encumbrance accounting is used to support budgetary control, accountability, and effective cash planning throughout the fiscal year.

Modified Accrual Basis

The modified accrual basis of accounting is employed for both budgeting purposes and audited financial statements for all governmental funds.

Revenues are recognized when measurable and available, while expenditures are recognized when liabilities are incurred.

Grant Revenue Treatment

Grant revenue is generally not included in the proposed or adopted budget until contractual grant agreements are executed.

Budget amendments are used throughout the fiscal year to recognize grant revenues and carry forward prior-year grant funding balances.

Phase One

Budget Preparation

The budget preparation phase establishes the foundation for the County’s annual financial planning process and provides departments with guidance for developing operational and capital budget requests.

Department Budget Development

The budget process begins with the widespread distribution of budget instructions to all County departments by the Office of Management & Budget. These instructions provide guidance for preparing and submitting departmental budget proposals.

During this phase, department directors and division heads evaluate current programs, review service levels, identify operational needs, and recommend organizational or methodological improvements.

Constitutional Officer Budgets

Constitutional Officers present proposed operating budgets for the upcoming fiscal year to the Board of County Commissioners at various stages of the process.

The Department of Revenue maintains final authority over the operating budgets of the Tax Collector and Property Appraiser.

Phase Two

Budget Review

During the review phase, the Office of Management & Budget evaluates departmental requests, analyzes operational priorities, and collaborates with County leadership to ensure proposed expenditures align with available resources and strategic service goals.

Comprehensive Budget Analysis

Upon receipt of budget requests, the Office of Management & Budget conducts a detailed examination of all capital and operating expense submissions from County departments.

Each request is carefully analyzed in relation to operational needs, projected revenues, service expectations, and available financial resources to ensure responsible fiscal planning.

Department Collaboration

OMB engages in periodic meetings with department directors and division leaders to review requests, discuss operational priorities, and evaluate proposed funding needs.

Administrative Review

The County Administrator and OMB host staff meetings dedicated to reviewing departmental appeals and supporting collaborative decision-making throughout the review process.

Review Objectives

  • Evaluate operational requests
  • Analyze funding priorities
  • Review departmental appeals
  • Align expenditures with revenues
  • Support transparent decision-making
Phase Three

Budget Workshop

The budget workshop is a key public step in the annual budget process, providing an opportunity for the preliminary budget to be presented, discussed, and refined before final adoption.

Public Budget Presentation

The Office of Management & Budget, in collaboration with department heads and the County Administrator, prepares for the public budget workshop.

This workshop allows the preliminary budget to be presented to the Board of County Commissioners and the community, creating a formal opportunity for review, discussion, and public feedback.

Community Input & Refinement

Public input gathered during the workshop helps guide refinements to the budget proposal, including discussion of service levels, funding priorities, and the proposed millage rate.

This step reinforces Walton County’s commitment to transparency, accountability, and community engagement throughout the budgeting process.

Phase Four

Budget Adoption

The budget adoption phase finalizes the County’s annual financial plan through public hearings, statutory compliance, and formal approval of the millage rate and operating budget.

Adoption Video

Budget Adoption Process

Budget Refinement & Certification

Following the public budget workshop, the Office of Management & Budget carefully updates the proposed budget to reflect revisions, community feedback, and policy direction provided by the Board of County Commissioners.

Once finalized, the proposed millage rates are certified to the Property Appraiser in accordance with Florida Statutes.

Public Hearings & Final Adoption

In August, the Property Appraiser distributes TRIM Notices to property owners throughout Walton County, informing taxpayers of proposed property tax rates and hearing information.

During September, the Board of County Commissioners conducts two public hearings and publishes required budget advertisements to ensure transparency and public awareness.

At the final public hearing, resolutions are adopted officially approving the millage rate and annual operating budget.

Budget Implementation

After adoption, the budget remains an active financial management tool. The Office of Management & Budget and County departments monitor spending, revenues, appropriations, and amendments throughout the fiscal year.

Ongoing Budget Monitoring

The budget process continues beyond legal adoption through year-round monitoring by the Office of Management & Budget and County departments.

This monitoring is supported by an integrated financial software system that provides budgetary control tools and reporting to assist with fiscal oversight.

Budgetary Control & Flexibility

To maintain fiscal discipline and operational flexibility, management may transfer budgeted appropriations between line accounts as authorized by statute and Board-approved policy.

Budgetary control is established at the fund level for all funds, ensuring that total fund appropriations remain within approved limits.

Budget Amendments

When circumstances require changes to the approved budget, the Board of County Commissioners may amend the budget by resolution in accordance with Section 129.06, Florida Statutes.

Amendments may be needed for unforeseen expenditures, use of contingency reserves, or allocation of unanticipated revenues.

Community Engagement

Public Involvement

Public participation plays an important role in Walton County’s annual budget process by providing residents with opportunities to review, discuss, and provide feedback on budget priorities and proposed service levels.

Public Budget Workshop

In Walton County, the public plays a critical role in the budget process, particularly during the public budget workshop typically held in July.

This workshop serves as the focal point for community engagement and provides residents with the opportunity to directly interact with the Board of County Commissioners.

Community Feedback & Participation

Discussions during the workshop focus on proposed service levels, funding priorities, and allocation of resources within the tentative budget.

Citizens are encouraged to ask questions, express concerns, provide recommendations, and offer feedback regarding how the County’s budget can best support community needs and priorities.

Supporting Transparent Decision-Making

Input gathered during the public workshop is considered by the Board throughout the budget process and contributes to the refinement and finalization of the County’s annual budget.
